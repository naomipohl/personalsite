Political Calendar App (FiveThirtyNine): Heroku link, Write-Up

15 Dec, 2018

This was a 4-person group project for CIS550 at Penn (Database & Information Systems). Our project, FiveThirtyNine, aimed to organize economic data, national political indicators (including President Trump’s approval rating and generic ballot party preferences), and top news headlines all in one easy-to-use to use platform. Meant for political junkies and casual observers alike, FiveThirtyNine has a variety of features that allow the user to study the national trends that defined 2018. The calendar feature provides data on stock indices, top news headlines, twitter trends, and political measurements for each day in 2018, allowing the user to explore at their own pace. We also wanted users to be able to dive into the data more deeply, so we created a functionality that allows users to search keywords such as “Hurricane” or “Saudi Arabia” to see how President Trump’s approval rating changed the last time these topics were in the national discussion. Users can also pick a range of dates for which they can see the dataset of their choice visualized graphically, or see a wordcloud composed of the news headlines and Twitter trends in that timeframe. Finally, users can explore what occurred during the most volatile days of Trump’s presidency, and directly see how national news events impact the political players in our democratic system.

Graph Property-Checker: Heroku link , Github link

28 Nov, 2017

*The app might take ~15 seconds to load (the server goes to sleep after a few minutes of inactivity).*

In my discrete math class this semester, instead of a second midterm we were assigned an open-ended project.
In class, we'd learned how to determine whether graphs have Euler paths/cycles and Hamiltonian circuits by hand, as well as how to tell whether a graph is bipartite. I wanted to learn how a computer would approach these problems, so a few classmates and I put together this Flask app. We used python for the back-end algorithms and javascript/HTML for the front-end jazz.
With our app, a user can draw a graph and click a button to find out whether the graph has certain properties. This was my first project with Flask, and yes, it really is as straightforward as they say it is!
